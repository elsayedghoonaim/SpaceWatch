The Growing Threat of Space
Collisions

In an era of unprecedented satellite proliferation, the threat of orbital collisions has escalated dramatically. Space debris, defunct satellites, and active spacecraft share increasingly crowded orbital pathways, posing a significant risk to vital space infrastructure. A single collision can generate thousands of new debris fragments, cascading into further collisions and rendering critical orbital regions unusable for generations. SpaceWatch provides the essential intelligence to navigate this complex environment, ensuring the safety and sustainability of space operations.


  • Over 30,000 tracked objects Actively monitored in Earth's orbit.
  • About 26,000 tracked objects currently in Earth's orbit
  • Collision speeds about 10-15 km/s relative velocity in orbit
  • Early detection can prevent catastrophic loss of critical infrastructure
