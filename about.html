About Rujum & Documentation

Understanding the technology and purpose behind Rujum.

Project Purpose

Rujum is a high-fidelity orbital tracking and collision avoidance system designed for satellite operators, researchers, and space agencies.

Our mission is to enhance space safety and sustainability by providing accurate, real-time predictions of satellite and debris trajectories, thereby minimizing collision risks and promoting safer space operations. We utilize the standard SGP4 propagation model, significantly enhanced with proprietary machine learning algorithms trained on historical tracking data. This hybrid approach allows for improved accuracy, especially for objects in complex orbital regimes or during periods of high solar activity.

Model Performance Across Time Horizons

Our advanced modeling capabilities ensure superior accuracy across various time horizons, providing reliable data for both immediate operational decisions and long-term strategic planning.

Short-Term Accuracy (7-20 Days)

For immediate conjunction analysis and maneuver planning, our ML model provides a critical accuracy boost over the standard SGP4, ensuring safer short-term operations. The graph demonstrates a significant reduction in error rates, highlighting the effectiveness of our machine learning enhancements.

Medium-Term Accuracy (1-12 Months)

While the current graph focuses on short-term accuracy,Rujum also provides robust long-term predictions, crucial for mission planning and orbital resource management. Our continuous research and development aim to further extend and refine these long-term capabilities.

Long-Term Accuracy (1-3 Years)

The ML-enhanced model is transformative for long-range ephemeris, reducing the Mean Absolute Error (MAE) by over 98% and enabling unprecedented catalog maintenance.

Organization


The Faculty of Navigation Science and Space Technology, a distinguished part of Beni Suef University, stands at the forefront of scientific and technological advancement in Egypt. Established with a forward-thinking vision, our faculty was created to address the growing demand for specialized expertise in the rapidly evolving fields of navigation and space technology. Since our inception, we have been committed to nurturing a vibrant academic environment that encourages intellectual curiosity, critical thinking, and practical application of knowledge. Our journey began with a clear objective: to build a robust foundation for future leaders and innovators in space and navigation. Over the years, we have continuously evolved our curriculum, integrated state-of-the-art facilities, and attracted a cadre of dedicated and highly qualified faculty members. This commitment to excellence has enabled us to produce graduates who are not only well-versed in theoretical concepts but are also proficient in applying their skills to real-world challenges, contributing to both national development and global scientific endeavors.
